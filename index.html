My Story

A letter from Nayanika:

Optional soundtrack while you read

I've struggled a lot to find my place in this field. I never formally studied neuroscience, either before or after deciding to pursue it. I taught myself everything I know through textbooks, research papers, and by cold-emailing people.

That made the journey difficult, but it also taught me things that no class ever could. Within two years of pivoting to neuroscience, I led my first research project. Along the way, I got to interact with researchers, clinicians, consumers, investors, and individuals with lived experience.

I wore many hats: researcher, communicator, advisor. I explored several subfields including computational, cognitive, behavioral, and systems neuroscience. Life has been a roller coaster.

But the hardest part of it all was the isolation. My story felt very different from those around me. Then I realized that almost every person I admire in neuroscience has a story like mine. That's just the nature of science. But the people who seemed to enjoy the process most had one thing in common: they had a community.

That's why I created Unconventional Neuroscientists.

You Don't Have to Do It Alone

I want to give you the kind of community I didn't have. Neuroscience isn't something people naturally fall into. It's almost always a nonlinear journey. But having people around you makes it okay when things don't make sense.

Our Vision

Safe Spaces for Real Talk

Large communities often don't feel intimate enough to share personal struggles. I want to change that. In this space, you should feel comfortable having a bad day, facing a tough decision, or feeling stuck, and being able to talk about it openly.

Discover Your Unique Path

Neuroscience is an enormous field. No one can know or do everything. This community is a place to explore what a neuroscience career means to you, based on your strengths, your interests, and your pace.

Innovate Beyond Boundaries

Let's build together. Whether you're interested in tools, ideas, content, or collaborations, this is a space to experiment and innovate at the intersection of disciplines and lived experiences.
